Medellin

Party in Medellin never stops. We found out that we party every second day till morning. It remainded me my home city, Prague. We've liked the city so much, that we considered an option to move to Medellin.
"Paisas" (people from Medellin) are friendly and beautiful people, they will make you feel good in their city. They know that they are carrying the stigma of violent city, controlled by drug cartells from the past, but they don't like to talk about it. They even don't want to say the name of Pablo Escobar. So when they want to mention him, they give him a nick name or they just don't say it. It was like in Harry Potter with Lord Voldemort sometimes:) But the city is now pretty safe and tourists are slowly coming back. They are so happy to see the tourists that they even stop you on the street, shake your hand and ask where are you from.

It was our second day in the city. We were in Parque Arvi, a large natural park on the top of the hill. In the past was very dangerous place, controlled by guerillas. Now it is a getaway from the busy city for locals and also children come to visit the park with their schools. That day was a vacation so there were large groups of children and young students. They approched us, gave us high five or told us hi. One girl then came and asked me where I am from. So I answered, but she wasn't really interested in the answer and with smile asked me "Selfie"?:)

About

Medellin is the second largest city of Colombia. Located in mountains it was pretty isolated from world. In 2003 was Medellin the most violent city in the world. In 2013 was Medellin awarded as the most innovative city in the world. Certainly building of metro, universities and also libraries in the poorest parts of the city helped Medellin to fight with criminality. Now there are still places where you don't want to go after dark, generally in the downtown, but it shouldn't discourage you from visit. Just use your common sence and trust your guts.

Transport

You can fly or you can go by bus. We went by bus from Santa Marta, it took us 19 hours and cost 160.000 COP for both of us. Don't forget that you can bargain on the counter, we begun on 200.000 COP. Flight ticket would cost 250.000 COP p.p. so we safe great money. The bus was very comfortable, lot of space for legs, adjustable and comfy seats, wi-fi and a toilet. Just one thing was just wrong, we were almost freezing. Wear a sweater, trousers, jacket and some blanket and you will be fain. We went with Brasilia company www.expresobrasilia.com

Attractions

Metrocable and Parque Arvi

Take a metro to the station Acevedo and then to change for cable metro. The ticket for metro cost us 2.000 COP and for cable metro 4.500 p.p. each way. It takes you up the hill, over houses made just from bricks and metal pieces. You will see almost whole city. The ride takes about 15 minutes.
