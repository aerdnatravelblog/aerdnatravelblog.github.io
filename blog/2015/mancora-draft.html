Mancora

We just arrived to this beach town and we wanted to try some street food. We crossed the town two times and we couldn't find anything. On the end, after the dark came, we found one woman cooking meat and rice. Very special on the coast of South America, so special that you almost don't eat anything else being there. But we were like "what the heck, let's try it". We ordered two plates and we were...not pleased by the flavors. Older man, smoking his cigarette, obviously not Peruvian noticed it. He came closer and ask us how is the food. We said that not good but that it is fine. He started to laugh and told us stories about social weak locals, who sometimes cook a rat or a cat. "Thank you oldie and nice to meet you!" That didn't help us at all of course. We fed with the rest of the food local dog, hopefully he survived.
The positive side of the thing was that he introduced us to Coco, very good surfing instructor. Next day we were being smashed by waves, doing our best to stand up for first time on the board. Coco was restless and every time helped us to get to the right place where we could catch the wave. Even another day when we were in the water on our own, not paying him, he was helping us. When we saw other instructors we were very happy to have Coco. Other people weren't so lucky and their instructors didn't pay them that much attention. Conclusion? We maybe ate the most disgusting food ever but it helped us create one of the best memories from our trip. So people, step from your comfort zone and eat disgusting food and talk to drunk old strangers without prejudices. It could lead you to life changing moments.

About

This was one of our favorite places in Peru. Because of the ocean, surfing, really friendly people, warm water and the perfect weather. In Mancora is almost always sunny and average temperature is about 27 degrees. In Montanita which is 500 km away is often cloudy and even rains. We weren't happy with the weather in Montanita at all, other travelers told us that in Peru the weather is better. They were definitely right. The only con is that the nightlife is not that great like in Montanita.
This hippie village is located in the north of Peru. We read everywhere how the place is expensive. Maybe it could be expensive if you would eat just in expensive restaurants and live in exclusive hotel. The true is that you can find very cheap hostels and menu del dia in every restaurant. For anything else we recommend to bargain. Especially with moto taxis, they want always double or even triple prices from tourists. We liked this place so much that we spent one week in very nice hostel Kon Tikki. This is the right place to take vacation from your vacation and have a rest after long term traveling. And you can see Iguanas. They are well hidden behind the handcraft market in bushes. Take bananas and go to visit them. When you will throw them few bananas they will come to you.

How to get to Mancora

We took a night bus from Cuenca directly to Mancora and we crossed the boarders to Peru. Don't worry about doing it night, it felt completely safe and fast. We left Cuenca at 9:00 pm and we were at Mancora at 4:30 am the next day. It cost us 15 USD.

Attractions

Water sports

The city beach in Mancora doesn't have the best conditions for surfing. There is one place, not large, where you can catch a wave. Obviously everybody is there and it can get overcrowded. Around Mancora are many beaches where you can surf or kite surf. Lobitos should have excellent surfing conditions but be aware that you need a wet suite as the water is really cold. There is a diferrent current south from Cabo Blanco (just 35 km south from Mancora), the Humboldt current. Because of stable and very strong wind it is a kite surfers paradise. Kite surfing is not so popular yet, so you have a lot of space.
We took lessons with Coco, unfortunately we forgot the name of the school. You can find it on the beach boulevard. If you ask on the beach in one of the surfing schools for him, you will find him. For 50 PEN (15 USD) you got two hours lesson, wet suit and board what you can then keep for rest of the day. They also rent surf boards for 20 PEN.

http://www.vivamancora.com/english/surf.htm

Swimming with sea turtles

There are so many tourist agencies selling this trip for 90 PEN or more. It is possible to do it on your own for 42 PEN for two people. Take a bus from terminal to Organos and then moto taxi to Nuro. The bus costs 2 PEN p.p. one way, moto taxi 10 PEN each way. To reach the turtles you have to pay the entrance, it is just 5 PEN. They are feeding them so they come close and they are very used to people obviously. You can jump to the water and swim with them for a while. Andrea rented a snorkel for 5 PEN and went snorkeling. Everywhere were signs to not touch them and there was security looking after the people and watching if they are behaving good to the animals. "I even couldn't move because there were five huge turtles around me. I didn't want to touch them. But they were touching me all the time!:)" Absolutely unique experience.

Exploring surrounding beaches

The beach in the city is not bad but there are much nicer beaches around. Like Pocitas beach, our favourite. Take a moto taxi for 5 PEN. We visited the beach in their winter and we had the beach for ourselves. The sand is very fine and water is clean. There are no bars and any shops so pack a piknik with you. The waves can get strong and big, we had so much fun jumping into them like kids. Stay till the sun set, it is spectacular.
Another beaches around are for example Organos or Cabo Blanco. We visited Organos (take the bus from terminal for 2 PEN), but the beach was dirty so we didn't stay long.

Food

La Gran Piadina

This was our favorite restaurant in Mancora. Almost every day we came for menu del dia. We tried everything what they have on menu. For 15 PEN you get a salad or bread with garlic, main course and a juice. Everything was always fresh and delicious. We loved their tuna steak with spaghetti al pesto or crispy chicken with mashed potatoes. Finally no rice but really good seasoned food with a taste. They open from 12 o'clock till 22:00. Adress:Av. Piura 245

https://www.facebook.com/Lagranpiadinamancora2013

Bananas cafe

This place is perfect for good and cheap breakfast. They have sandwiches, eggs and even a salad with piece of cheese. We liked Bananas combo breakfast which consists from a salad: piece of avocado, tomatoes, cheese and focaccia with olive oil; small yogurt with fruit, fresh juice and coffee or tea for 10 PEN. Continental costs 6 PEN. Adress:Av. Piura 224

http://www.vivamancora.com/bananascafe/#_=_

The Market

If you want good and fresh ceviche, go in the day to the market. There are five or six stalls next to each other serving this peruvian specialty. We tried the one with black mussels "conchas negras". It was one of the best what we've tried in Peru. They serves it with a sweet potato, steamed corn and crunchy fried corn. Costs 15 PEN.

Where to stay

Kon Tikki hostel

This hostel is not directly in Mancora, but up hill from the city, overlooking it. Far enough from the smog and noise. They offer bungalows, we choose the cheapest option for 35 PEN, double room per night with shared bathroom. Bathroom was always clean and shower strong enough. The advantage of bungalow is that you have privacy and you have a terrace and a hammock. We spend afternoons reading books, drinking beer and looking at the sea from our gorgeous view point. You can also use the kitchen and big social area with sofas, tables and chairs. The architecture of the hostel with white buildings remembered us Spanish villages called "pueblos blancos". We stayed one week and we would come back to Mancora we would stay again in this hostel. Btw the wifi was good, working better at the terrace.

Nightlife

In the week there is not that much going on. On the weekend starts all the beach bar pulling out the speakers and play very loud music, very bad international music usually. However young people from Mancora are very nice, friendly and open minded. They like to talk to tourists and share beers. On the end we didn't care that they play shitty music, we danced under the stars with legs soaking in sea water and had fun with tons of nice people. In Atelier club, av. Piura, play often house or techno music.
